Community Spaces

Every neighbourhood in Naawi Oodena is woven together by publicly accessible parks, plazas, mews, and courtyards. These spaces are designed for year round use, featuring sheltered seating, evening lighting, and flexible hardscapes for markets, ceremonies, or spontaneous recreation.

Cultural Campus & Education

Naawi-Oodena Cultural Campus is a space for celebration, learning and gathering around Indigenous cultures. It has a public library, a museum, an art gallery, a public performance space, and the Treaty One Nation Governance Center. Parking is out of sight of the campus, keeping it open and welcoming, and the campus is networked with bike paths and accommodating walkways.

Sports & Recreation

Naawi-Oodena is A vibrant,year-round recreational community of community, culture and wellness. The large outdoor fields are used for baseball, soccer, and for cultural events, and the powwow arbour is used for festivals and ceremonies. Winter brings landscape changes, with nearby hills open for sledding and snow play and stormwater ponds converting to skating rinks. The entire area is linked by paths for cycling and walking, is designed to promote movement, socializing and even celebration throughout the year.
